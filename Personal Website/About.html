    • School Life/Service

    Currently, I am in my second semester of my junior year here at Texas A&M. I have finished most all of my basics with the exception of business courses and am solely focused on computer science. On any given day, I spend anywhere between 2-5 hours coding, with the times fluctuating more on the weekend depending on my workload. Much of this time is spent on school specific projects as I am in three coding-heavy courses: Intro to Computer Systems, Design and Analysis of Algorithms, and Programming Studio. Although this schedule leaves me with little freetime I rarely feel bogged down as I enjoy the work. If anything, this semester has and continues to show me how much I have left to learn, and how I am capable of more than I previously thought. My other two classes are a seminar class that meets up once a week and listens to guest speakers in industry give their experience, and an online management class for my business emphasis area.

    Other than classes I am involved in two clubs: CyberSecurity and Texas A&M Computing Society. Both clubs are similar to my seminar class as they welcome speakers to give speeches in a more casual setting. Unfortunately, my involvment in both clubs has seen a decrease this and last semester as my courseload has become more time-consuming.

    Personal Life

    I was born in Houston, Texas but moved to and currently live in Katy, Texas, a rapidly growing town west of Houston. I went to Seven Lakes High School where I joined the marching band playing percussion. Band was a passion of mine that led me to make life-long friends and eventually meet my girlfriend at the end of my senior year. After graduating I kept in contact with my old instructor and helped teach at George Ranch High School, George Bush High School, and North Shore High School. For the past 7-8 months I've held a job at Under Armour back in my hometown, taking extended breaks during the school year. In my freetime I enjoy working out, cooking, and outdoor activities. Ever since entering college I decided to take my health more serious, especially because of the sedentary lifestyle of coding.

    My time here at A&M has brought along with it some great memories. Although I am not as involved as other students in organizations I still have made many friends in my classes and around campus that have made the decision to come here worth it. I hope to carry the traditions and values of a true Aggie everywhere I go in the future.
