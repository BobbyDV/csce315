    Goals in Computer Science

    I was first introduced to computer science my senior year of high school and immediately felt a connection. The past three years have only solidified that connection with various school projects and outside learning. Although I have made progress in the last three years, I want to venture out of my comfort zone in relation to the projects and languages/programs I learn in and out of the classroom. Texas A&M is an amazing school for computer science, but my classes can only do so much on their own. My immediate goal is to get a co-op or internship to break through to the next level in the industry; to get an idea of the kind of work I may be doing for the next few years, or of an area that I may ultimately not have much interest in so I can focus on others. The kind of work I find fulfilling is the kind that makes a difference in people's lives. It may not be the biggest difference, but the greatest gift of technology is the ease of life it provides. To be a part of that process in a meaningful way makes what I do everyday worth the time and work put in. While this is happening, I will continue to improve as a programmer, especially outside of my classes. My next steps are to solidify my expertise of the more advanced and data structures; being able to demonstrate my knowledge in personal projects. As I already know C++, Java, and Python, I feel moving on to scripting languages and cloud-based services will round out my skills. This upcoming summer, beginning in May, I hope to finish all the goals I have set for myself and more. Thank you for reading and stay tuned for any updates in the future.
